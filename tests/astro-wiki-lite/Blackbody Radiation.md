# Blackbody Radiation

A blackbody is an idealized object that absorbs all incident radiation and
re-emits a characteristic thermal spectrum determined solely by its temperature.
Stars are decent approximations to blackbodies over much of their spectrum.

## Planck's Law

Planck's law gives the spectral radiance of a blackbody as a function of
temperature and wavelength. Integrating it over all wavelengths yields the
Stefan–Boltzmann law; its peak location gives Wien's displacement law.

## Why it matters

The blackbody spectrum sets the baseline against which emission and absorption
lines are measured, and its temperature dependence lets us infer stellar surface
temperatures from broad-band colors.
