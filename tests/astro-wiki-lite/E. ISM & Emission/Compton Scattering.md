# Compton Scattering

Compton scattering is the inelastic scattering of a photon by a charged
particle, usually a free electron. The photon transfers part of its energy to
the electron, so the scattered photon comes away with a longer wavelength.

## Wavelength shift

The change in wavelength depends only on the scattering angle, not on the
incident wavelength. The shift is largest for back-scattering, where the photon
reverses direction and gives up the most energy to the electron.

## Inverse Compton

In **inverse Compton scattering** the roles flip: a low-energy photon gains
energy from a relativistic electron. This up-scattering is a major source of
X-rays and gamma-rays in the interstellar medium and in AGN jets, and it drives
the Sunyaev–Zel'dovich effect in galaxy clusters.
